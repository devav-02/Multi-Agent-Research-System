## AI definition

Artificial intelligence (AI) refers to computer systems or models that are designed to perform tasks that normally require human thinking, such as understanding language, recognizing patterns, solving problems, and making decisions. Traditional AI systems follow only fixed rules, but newer AI systems, like [cognitive AI](https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/resources/cloud-computing-dictionary/what-is-cognitive-ai), can learn from data and improve over time, which allows them to become more accurate in handling new situations.

AI is used in many of our everyday tools, including virtual assistants, recommendation systems, and chatbots.

- AI models identify patterns and relationships in data that can be used to generate new predictions, recommendations, and responses.
- Organizations across major industries are already using AI to boost productivity and drive innovation in real-world use cases.
- Looking to the future, AI is expected to expand opportunities, contribute to economic growth, and foster long-term innovation.

### How AI works

AI models built on the [generative pre-trained transformer (GPT)](https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/resources/cloud-computing-dictionary/what-is-gpt) use [machine learning](https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/resources/cloud-computing-dictionary/artificial-intelligence-vs-machine-learning) to analyze large amounts of data, such as text, images, or numbers, identifying patterns and relationships that can be used to produce outputs, such as predictions, recommendations, or responses.

The process typically follows a simple cycle:

1. Data is collected and prepared
2. The model is trained to recognize patterns
3. Results are tested and improved through feedback

With each cycle, the AI model adjusts itself, becoming more accurate and capable of handling new situations more effectively. Essentially, AI works by continuously learning from data, refining its understanding, and applying that learning to deliver better results over time.

# Key takeaways

Let's break AI down in a way that everyone can understand.

- **What it is**: AI is a field of computer science focused on creating smart machines that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, like learning, reasoning, and problem-solving.

# What is Artificial intelligence (AI)?

Artificial intelligence (AI) encompasses the fields of computer and [data science](https://www.mtu.edu/data-science/undergraduate/data-science/what-is/) focused on building machines with human intelligence to perform tasks like learning,
reasoning, problem-solving, perception, and language understanding. Instead of relying
on explicit instructions from a programmer, AI systems learn from data that enables
them to handle complex problems and simple repetitive tasks, improving how they respond
over time.

AI handles many tasks faster with exceptional accuracy and reliability, freeing humans
from repetitive and tedious chores. Businesses and organizations save time and money
by automating and optimizing routine processes, using the technology to stay connected
with customers and gain a competitive edge.

AI is part of everyday life. If you've used a self-service kiosk to check in before
a flight, typed keywords into a search bar and received suggested results, or communicated
with a digital assistant, you've interacted with AI.

## Fundamental Components of AI

- **Data:** AI systems learn and make decisions based on data, and they require large quantities
of information to train effectively, especially for machine learning (ML) models.
- **Algorithms:** Algorithms are the sets of rules AI systems use to process data and make decisions.
Machine learning algorithms, for instance, learn and make predictions and decisions
without explicit programming.
- **Computing power:** AI algorithms often need significant computing resources to process large quantities
of data and run complex algorithms.
